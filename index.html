Red Blood Cell Count

Count RBCs in 5 small squares (each 0.04 mm²) of the central large square on the Neubauer chamber.

Dilution Factor
Formula: RBC (×10⁶/µL) = (Total cells in 5 squares ÷ 5) × Dilution × (1 ÷ 0.004 µL) ÷ 1,000,000
Simplified: RBC = Average count per square × Dilution × 250 ÷ 1,000,000
Volume of 1 small square: 0.2mm × 0.2mm × 0.1mm = 0.004 mm³ = 0.004 µL

White Blood Cell Count

Count WBCs in all 4 large corner squares (each 1mm × 1mm × 0.1mm = 0.1 µL) of the Neubauer chamber.

Dilution Factor
Formula: WBC (×10³/µL) = (Total cells in 4 squares ÷ 4) × Dilution × 10 ÷ 1,000
Simplified: WBC = Average count × Dilution ÷ 100
Volume of 4 large squares: 4 × (1mm × 1mm × 0.1mm) = 0.4 µL
